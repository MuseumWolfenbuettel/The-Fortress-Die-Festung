The Fortress – Die Festung

The settled areas in the marshy depression in the river were built into a fortified town to defend the capital of the small principality along with its residents and their possessions in times of war. Surrounded by the Oker and a diverting bypass channel, people sought protection behind ramparts and fortifications. Although, in the 17th century, the fortress was considered one of the strongest in northern Germany, invulnerable to gunpowder and armaments, the imperial field marshal Gottfried Heinrich Graf zu Pappenheim managed to capture it during the Thirty Years' War. He dammed the Oker and flooded the town, forcing it to capitulate in 1627.
